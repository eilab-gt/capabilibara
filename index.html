Abstract

Training data as a capability map.

We use training-data attribution as an interpretable tool for capability discovery, mapping which regions of the pretraining corpus support social reasoning versus STEM reasoning in OLMo3-7B.

Document-level attribution scores are too noisy to interpret directly, so we aggregate them through WebOrganizer's 24 topic by 24 format taxonomy over the de-duplicated Dolma3 corpus. The result is a 576 by 4 bin-level influence matrix across SocialIQA, MMLU Social Sciences, ARC-Challenge, and MMLU STEM.

Social and STEM reasoning draw on distinct corpus regions, and the split is sharper for reasoning than for knowledge. Targeted unlearning provides partial causal validation: forgetting high-attribution social regions degrades SocialIQA more than matched random controls while largely preserving ARC-Challenge.

Method

From documents to interpretable corpus regions.

The central move is aggregation: every benchmark query is traced back to many individual documents, then summarized into comparable topic-format regions.

Corpus taxonomy

24 topics crossed with 24 formats.

Dolma3 is de-duplicated, classified, and sampled into 10,000 documents per occupied bin. This turns attribution from a document retrieval problem into a structured corpus comparison problem.

Contrastive design

Domain and capability type are separated.

The 2 by 2 design asks whether social reasoning is merely social-domain knowledge, or whether reasoning tasks use different corpus regions than knowledge tasks in the same domain.

The answer is mostly the latter: capability type differentiates the corpus more strongly than domain alone.

Key Findings

Social reasoning has a distinct provenance signature.

01

SocialIQA is the outlier.

Its 576-bin profile correlates with each comparison benchmark at only r ≤ 0.22, compared with r = 0.53-0.86 among the other three tasks.

02

The signature bin sign-flips.

Literature x Customer Support is strongly supportive for SocialIQA (z = +16.0), but suppressive for MMLU Social Sciences (z = -7.31) and MMLU STEM (z = -5.75).

03

Reasoning separates more than knowledge.

Social-STEM differences are sharper for reasoning tasks than knowledge tasks, with an approximately 1.7x wider contrast range.

04

Unlearning gives a selective damage profile.

Influence-targeted forgetting degrades SocialIQA more than same-topic random controls most clearly, while ARC-Challenge is largely preserved or reversed.

Lexical signature

The top SocialIQA bins split into two text regimes.

Short dialogue-heavy interpersonal text and long technical documentation both appear in high-influence regions, suggesting social reasoning is not explained by a single genre.
