Abstract

We use training-data attribution as an interpretable tool for capability discovery, mapping which regions of the pretraining corpus support social-reasoning versus STEM-reasoning in OLMo3-7B. Training-data attribution measures how strongly each training document influences a model's predictions on a benchmark, but document-level scores are too noisy to identify which corpus regions support which capabilities, and prior work has emphasized factual knowledge rather than reasoning.

We compute gradient-based attribution (TrackStar via Bergson) over a working set drawn from the de-duplicated Dolma3 mix, aggregate influence across WebOrganizer's 24-format × 24-topic taxonomy (576 bins), and contrast benchmark pairs in a 2×2 design that varies domain (social vs. STEM) and capability type (reasoning vs. knowledge): SocialIQA and MMLU Social Sciences against ARC-Challenge and MMLU STEM.

Social and STEM reasoning draw on qualitatively distinct corpus regions, and the contrast is sharper at the reasoning level than at the knowledge level. Targeted machine unlearning provides partial causal validation: forgetting high-attribution topic bins (e.g., Literature for SocialIQA) degrades the aligned benchmark more than within-bin random baselines, and we will release audited code, sampling manifests, the bin-level influence matrix, and unlearning checkpoints after the public release review.

Key Findings

Within the OLMo3-7B / Dolma3 setting, a fixed taxonomy turns noisy document-level scores into corpus units that can be compared, inspected, and perturbed.

SocialIQA is the outlier

Its 576-bin profile correlates with each comparison benchmark at only r ≤ 0.22, versus r = 0.53–0.86 among the other three. Its influence is diffuse and narrative/interpersonal (top formats: Customer Support, FAQ, Q&A Forum).

The signature bin sign-flips

Literature × Customer Support is SocialIQA's highest-influence bin (z = +16.0) yet flips strongly negative for MMLU Social Sciences (−7.31) and MMLU STEM (−5.75).

Reasoning > domain

The social–STEM split is sharper for reasoning than for knowledge (~1.7× wider Δz range). Capability type is more corpus-differentiating than domain.

Bimodal lexical signature

SocialIQA's top bins split into short, dialogue-rich interpersonal text and long-form technical documentation — mean document length differs >20× across the two clusters.

Unlearning validates SocialIQA

Influence-targeted forgetting degrades accuracy more than same-topic random controls most clearly for SocialIQA (Wilcoxon pBH < 10⁻⁴) — a selective damage profile, weaker/null/reversed for the others.

Knowledge is concentrated

High-influence bins correlate more tightly with accuracy drop for knowledge benchmarks than for reasoning — knowledge rests on a few load-bearing bins; reasoning is robust to single-bin removal.

Methodology

Four components take corpus-scale gradient attribution to an interpretable, causally-testable instrument.

1 · Open Ecosystem

OLMo3-7B (Base + Instruct) over the Dolma3 corpus. Document gradients/curvature are computed on Base (tied to pretraining data); benchmark query gradients on Instruct (follows OLMES multiple-choice prompts more reliably).

2 · Corpus & Taxonomy

The 6T-token Dolma3 mix is de-duplicated (~67% duplication) to ~1.26B unique documents, then classified with WebOrganizer into 24 topics × 24 formats = 576 bins. A stratified working set samples 10k docs/bin (5.68M docs) to counter corpus skew.

3 · Gradient Attribution

Gradient-based TDA using TrackStar via Bergson v0.9.0, projecting gradients into a low-rank subspace to run at corpus scale. Mean influence per (benchmark, bin) yields a 576×4 influence matrix, z-standardized within benchmark, with a 1,000-iteration bootstrap noise floor for the contrastive Δz.

4 · Unlearning Validation

Rank-8 LoRA adapters trained with the gradient-normalized NGDiff objective, across selection strategy (random vs. influence-guided) × scope (single-topic vs. global). A global random control gives the net effect γnet = γtopic − γglobal.

Benchmarks & Corpus

The 2×2 design uses four uniformly multiple-choice benchmarks, crossing domain with capability type. (GSM8K and ARC-Easy were removed so the final design is format-matched, eliminating a format-vs-content confound.)

Social · Reasoning

  • SocialIQA — commonsense reasoning about social situations and intentions.

STEM · Reasoning

  • ARC-Challenge — hard grade-school science reasoning questions.

Social · Knowledge

  • MMLU Social Sciences — factual social-science knowledge.

STEM · Knowledge

  • MMLU STEM — factual science, technology, engineering & math knowledge.

🗂️ Corpus

  • Dolma3 — 6T-token mix → ~1.26B unique docs after de-duplication.
  • WebOrganizer taxonomy — 24 topics × 24 formats = 576 bins; ~10.5B tokens sampled.
